Usability testing methods

This or that

 

Highlighter test The technique to allow us to understand the impact of what we are writing. We're learning how to make our content clear and simple. How to do that? We ask participants to read a printout of content and to underline things that made them feel more confident about the service in green, and things that made them feel less confident in red.

This technique allows us to understand the impact of what we are writing. We're learning how to make our content clear and simple. How to do that? We ask participants to read a printout of content and to underline things that made them feel more confident about the service in green, and things that made them feel less confident in red. So the role of the participant is to highlight positive and negative words in the content and rate them from 1 to 5, depending on their importance. To do the test we need just two different color highlighters: red for negative and yellow for positive impact. The red text needs to be rewritten.

 

A/B or multivariate test Simplified A/B testing is used to show preferences (this or that). This type of test shows the customers' preferences and answers the question about what they would like to use. A/B testing allows targeting target your resources for maximum output, making as few modifications as possible.

Card sorting Card Sorting is a tool for exploring the “Mental Model” of participants. It means the way they search for information, products, or solutions.

Card Sorting is a tool for exploring the “Mental Model” of participants. It means the way they search for information, products, or solutions.

The person conducting the test (usability analyst, user experience designer, etc.) identifies key concepts and writes them on post-it notes. The role of testers is to arrange the cards (individually or as a group) to show how they see the structure and relationships of the information.

 

How to perform a card sort:

A personal representative of the audience is given a set of index cards with terms written on them.

This person groups the terms in whatever way they think is logical, and gives each group a category name. It can be picked from an existing card or written on a blank card.

Testers repeat this process across a group of test subjects.

The testers later analyze the results to discover patterns.

Google trends The tool you can use to follow what people search for. Google Trends gives access to the sample of actual search requests made to Google. It's anonymized (no one is personally identified), categorized (determining the topic for a search), and aggregated (grouped).
