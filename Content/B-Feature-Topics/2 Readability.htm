Readability

The first principle - simplicity

 

We want the content to be readable by its users. What does it mean? If the client has to read something again and again to understand it, the content quality needs to be improved. Users want to be able just to scan the text rather than reading every word. How to check it?

 

Readability testing can tell you how easy or difficult your content is to read. Readability tools check the readability of your writing by checking, for example:

  • sentences length

  • grammar

  • use of complex words.

 

Factors that can improve readability:

  • font-weight
  • font size
  • leading (something that is also called line-height)
  • line width
  • justification
  • color of text
  • capitalized or lower-case letters

 

Analysis of the content

There are testing tools that provide an analysis of the content. Readability tests give a statistical analysis of the difficulty of a text. A readability score can tell you what level of education someone will need to be able to read a piece of text easily.

 

There are a few factors that make your content readable.

  • the length of the sentences and paragraphs

The desirable line length for the web is 50-60 characters. Paragraphs include 3-4 sentences each.

 

  • precise information

Websites are not a piece of literature. Its purpose is to be precise information. Too much complex content is hard to understand. It will push the readers away.

 

  • simple words & text

Your reader is lazy. Too many technical or just unfamiliar terms can be overwhelming. If your content is tiring for customers, they migrate to the web pages easier to understand and absorb.

 

  • bullet points

Bulleted lists are a good example of crisp, clear, and easy information.
